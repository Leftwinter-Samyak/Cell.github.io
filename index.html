Cell

What is a cell ?

Cell is the structural and functional unit of life.All human beings are made up of cells.The size of the cells are measured in microns.The cell is divided into three parts - nucleus, which is the control centre of cell, cell membrane,which allows certain substances to enter in the cell like oxygen ( O2 ) and cytoplasm ,which occupies space between nucleus and cell membrane. The components present in the cell and floats on cytoplasm are called cell organelles.

Structure of a nucleus

The nucleus is surrounded by a double membrane called nuclear membrane . Inside the membrane , is a dense fluid called nucleoplasm . A network of thread like structures called chromatin is present on this nucleoplasm. These chromatins form small divisions at the time of cell division known as chromosomes which carry genes. The nucleus is the control centre of the cell and plays an important role in cell division.

Cell membrane

A cell membrane is a thin , delicate and flexible covering the cell. It controls the movement of materials in and out of the cell. It also protects the internal components of a cell.

Cytoplasm

Cytoplasm is a colourless , translucent fluid which occupies the space between the cell membrane and nucleus. It is composed of ions , various molecules like amino acids , carbohydrates , enzymes and different salts .

Who discovered cell ?

The cell was discovered and named by Robert Hooke(Click here to see his photo),an English scientist, in the year 1665 with the help of the compound microscope which he had invented to observe two different objects.He had born on 28th July 1635 at Freshwater in United Kingdom and was educated in Wadham College in London. He is well known for Hooke's law , microscopy and had coined the term cell. He died on 3rd March 1703 in London , United Kingdom. He found and coined the term cell when he observed small box-like structures while examining a cork under his compound microscope.

Different shapes of cell

There are many different shapes of cell . Some of them with their examples are -

(a) Oval : Chlamydomonas(a unicellular green alga)

(b) Irregular : Amoeba(a unicellular organism), white blood cell(a type of blood cell in our body)

(c) Oblong : Paramecium(a unicellular organism)

(d) Elongated : Muscles cells(a multicellular organism)

(e) Thread-like : Nerve cells(a multicellular organism)

(f) Cubical or rectangular : Leaf cell(a multicellular organism)

Different sizes of cell

The size of the cells are measured in micrometre or micron (μ) , which is equal to one-thousandth of a millimetre. The smallest cells are those of bacteria , which range from 0.2 to 0.5 μ in diameter. The smallest cells in human body are the red blood cells , which measures 6 - 8 μ. The largest cell is an ostrich's egg, which measures 23 cm. And the longest cells are the nerve cells of an elephant , which measures 3 m.

Unicellular and multicellular organisms

The organisms are divided in terms of number of cells. They are unicellular organisms and multicellular organisms. The unicellular organisms are the organisms which are made up of one cell and the multicellular organisms are organisms which are made up of millions and billions of cell.
